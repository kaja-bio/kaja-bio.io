THEME: TRADITIONS

Burger Day (working title)

INT. BURGER KING

A dilapidated Burger King in the apocalypse.

Two women are sitting across from each other at a table (Aisha and the Cleaner). They’re wearing slightly askew party hats. Between them is a can of beans. A man enters from the back, holding a plate with a burger - this is Ivan. Another woman (Madge) comes from behind him, clapping each time he steps. She looks miserable. He places down the plate when he reaches the table. They all gather around, make a simultaneous gesture, whisper “Burger Day”.

AISHA

Happy Burger Day, folks!

They all make the same simultaneous gesture and then whisper “Burger day”.

MADGE

This is still fucking stupid.

AISHA

Don’t be moody, Madge - it’s Burger Day today!

They all make the same simultaneous gesture and then whisper “Burger day”.

IVAN

(nodding) Burger Day good day.

They all make the same simultaneous gesture and then whisper “Burger day”.

AISHA

What are you wishing for, Ivan?

IVAN

Bigger knife. (He takes a chef knife out of the front pocket of his uniform). Old knife too small.

Aisha claps her hands and turns to Madge.

AISHA

What about you, Madge?

MADGE

I wish for us stop participating in this dumb tradition.

AISHA

Madge, that’s not constructive -

MADGE

Well, that’s what I wish for.

She folds her arms and sits down on the floor in a huff. Ivan fishes another party hat out of his pocket and puts it on Madge, patting her on the shoulder.

IVAN

Good day today, Madge. We serve Customer. We eat burger. We make prayer.

MADGE

What fucking customers-

AISHA

(cutting in) Madge, please. Let’s keep with the tone of the day, shall we?

IVAN

(nodding) Good day.

AISHA

And what about you, Cleaner? What would you like?

The cleaner stares at Aisha. She takes off her hat and starts tearing it apart. Aisha takes it from her and puts it back on her head.

AISHA

No, you’re doing it - you’re doing it wrong. (She sighs and smiles, trying to hold it together.) Well, do you know what I wish for?

IVAN

What Aisha wishing for?

AISHA

I wish for today to be a productive and prosperous day. I hope that we all serve the Customer to the best of our abilities. I pray that we clean when we have time to lean and that our smiles are always right there on our happy little faces!

She puts on a big smile.

IVAN

Happy Burger Day!

They all make the same simultaneous gesture and then whisper “Burger day”. They then lean forward, make another gesture (perhaps blow on the burger as if it was a birthday cake) and start tearing off pieces of the burger and eating it.

MADGE

(while chewing) Does it disturb anyone else that it’s been three years and it still tastes the same?

Cleaner nods. She takes off her hat again and starts chewing on that. Aisha grabs it from her.

AISHA

Well, Madge, of course it would taste the same. We’ve served the Customer well. You work well, you get rewarded. I’m afraid that’s just common sense.

She laughs. It sounds nervous. An alarm sounds. Cleaner jumps up and starts wiping nothing off the counters, mopping, etc.. Ivan goes and takes a chopping board and starts to chop nothing. Madge goes to a register. She smiles and turns to the audience.

MADGE

Hiya! What would you like to order?

She smiles and waits.

MADGE

It’ll be just a minute for the chips. (she turns and shouts to Ivan) Large chips and a cheeseburger! (she turns back to the audience).

IVAN

On it!

AISHA

Guys, don’t serve any of the Coke - we’re out. It’s only Pepsi on the menu now.

Another alarm sounds. Everyone freezes except for MADGE.

MADGE

(looking around nervously, then talking to the audience. She walks around the stage while talking, showing around the Burger King and the people there with her) It’s been a couple years now that I’ve lived in this Burger King. You’d think it’d be a pretty miserable place to be, but it’s actually sort of nice. The chairs are comfortable. There’s loads of food still in the pantry, even though the four of us have been eating for an eternity. And in this disturbing, strangling, eating-me-up and crushing me way, it feels like home.

(walking over to Aisha, adjusting her party hat) Aisha and Ivan like it best, I think. Although sometimes I think they hate it and the only one of us who loves it is the Cleaner, because she doesn’t seem to want to do anything but clean. She used to work at the pub down the road - I saw her down there, back before everything went to shit. Yknow. What with the apocalypse and all.

(She walks over to the cleaner) You would think that survival is the hardest part of the apocalypse, but it’s not. It’s the lack of routine. All of those books you read, about people finding courage and purpose and Their Inner Selves among the rubble of the wasted world?

Bunch of horse shit.

As soon as we were able to, what we yearned for most was to be cogs in the machine again. And so here we are. At the end of the day, it’s not even the warmth or the food or the shelter that keep us here. It’s the sense of place. We serve. We work together. We worship profit, even if money no longer exists. It’s an old, dead god, but we serve him anyway.

(madge pauses) But lately, I’ve been wondering what’s outside again.

Alarm. Everyone unfreezes and goes back to doing what they were doing before. CLEANER walks around mopping the floors, humming to herself. AISHA walks around with a notebook and a pen, taking stock. IVAN continues chopping, cooking, etc. MADGE looks out at the audience, semi-frozen.

AISHA

Madge, if you’re free right now, could you look over the broken till?

MADGE

All of the tills are broken, Aisha. Everything here is broken.

AISHA

(forced smiling) Madge, I’d really appreciate not having this conversation with you right now.

MADGE

(sarcastically) Yes chef.

MADGE fiddles around with a terminal. CLEANER comes up around her and, while IVAN and AISHA are distracted, slips a piece of paper into her pocket. MADGE whips around to look at her, but CLEANER has gone back to mopping/sweeping/etc. MADGE takes the piece of paper out of her pocket, reads it, then slips it back into her pocket.

INT. BURGER KING BATHROOM

MADGE is by the sink, looking in the mirror. She looks tired. CLEANER comes into the room and MADGE turns to look at her.

MADGE

What did you mean by the note?

CLEANER shrugs.

MADGE

(reading the note) “Routine hides insanity.” (looking at CLEANER) You know, it’s the first time we’ve ever spoken. Really spoken.

CLEANER nods.

MADGE

I mean, I know this counts, too. I know words aren’t the only way to communicate. But I miss words. I miss sane words. I miss words that mean something, words that aren’t a vehicle to convey the fact that we need to refill the Sprite. I miss poetry and theater. I miss the days that weren’t predictable, that weren’t routine, I miss getting to shirk tradition.

CLEANER steps up to MADGE, who’s still, throughout this whole thing, wearing her party hat. She gently takes off the party hat and tears off a piece, chews it up, spits it out, then offers the same to MADGE, who does the same.

MADGE

(spitting it out) I’m sorry I never learned your name.

At that moment, IVAN and AISHA burst in. AISHA looks angry, IVAN confused.

AISHA

Do you realize that you’re not the only person in the world? We can’t always be picking up your slack, Madge. There’s customers waiting.

MADGE

Aisha-

AISHA

(cutting her off, towards Cleaner) And you - what, you’re hiding away with her in the bathroom now? Have either of you heard the term ‘no man is an island’? Well, man isn’t an island - man is a piece. A beautiful chess piece, and with you two missing, the game is unplayable.

MADGE

I’m not a piece, Aisha. And neither is she.

AISHA

So what are you, then? A lonely woman hiding out in a Burger King because she’s too scared to confront the outside? Don’t you see how - how pathetic that is?

MADGE

(softly) Aisha. We have to leave sometime.

IVAN

Not today.

MADGE

So WHEN? Tomorrow? During the next batshit tradition we invent? After we’ve served all the imaginary customers and lived out our make-believe lives?

IVAN

It’s scary outside.

AISHA

Madge - it’s for your own good. You can’t go.

MADGE nods.

MADGE

For what it’s worth - I tried to love this life, Aisha.

She breaks out into a sprint suddenly. The other three follow her.

EXT. OUTSIDE

MADGE alone enters the stage again (maybe she faces the audience, maybe she walks off the stage and just before the audience. She might walk thru a door or smthn to signal that she’s outside). She falls to her knees.

MADGE

Oh my god. It’s -

END PLAY, LIGHTS OUT
