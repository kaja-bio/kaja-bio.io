THE ZOO

MAN, dressed in a jumpsuit-type outfit, addresses the audience.

MAN

Welcome, ladies and gentleman! My name is Man, and I’ll be your tour guide in the zoo today. I know you’ve all paid a hefty sum to be here today, and we appreciate your commitment to supporting our little business endeavor. Now, before we go in, I hope all of your cameras are off - (points at random person) I’m looking at you, miss. I’m not blind.

MAN starts laughing, slightly too long for comfort.

MAN

Imagine if I were blind! Why, I could just fit right into a zoo myself, couldn’t I?

MAN wipes an imaginary tear off his cheek, looking wistful.

MAN

Oh, we have fun together, don’t we? (sudden change, seriously, pointing at the person he pointed to before) But really, if I see you pointing that camera at anything, I won’t hesitate to throw you in a cage, too.

The curtains behind MAN open, revealing a “zoo”. There are various people crouching in cages, clad in business attire. This is the LANDLORD, the POLITICIAN and the BANKER.

MAN

Behold, ladies and gentlemen: the zoo!

 

MAN walks up to the LANDLORD, sitting with their legs crossed on the floor. He leans closer to the person.

MAN

Don’t be shy, now. Introduce yourself.

LANDLORD

My name is REDACTED. I am 34 years old. I was a landlord with property in London, Edinburgh and Glasgow.

MAN

A landlord! Isn’t that something.That’s right, my dear tour group, we have a variety of animals on display. Parasites, invertebrates, all the horned and scaled things of the world.

POLITICIAN

My name is -

MAN turns suddenly.

MAN

(shouting) Is it your turn to speak yet?

POLITICIAN shakes their head.

MAN

(addressing audience) Apologies. This doesn’t usually happen. Now, where were we? Our landlord. Right, where’s the damn assistant?

MAN looks around; seeing nobody, he turns to the side and yells.

MAN

Grigory!

Another man, GRIGORY, scuttles out from the side of the stage.

GRIGORY

Sorry, yes, sorry, sir. You wanted me?

MAN

(gesturing to audience) We have a tour group here, Grigory. They’d like to learn a bit more about the animals in our zoo, I think.

GRIGORY

Ah, yes, sorry… right, yes, ah... sorry, where was I?

MAN

The landlord.

GRIGORY

Ah, the landlord, yes, yes… The Landlord is typically found in an urban habitat. Their diet consists of… ah, let me look through my notes again.

MAN

Grigory, I told you to have learned it by heart this time!

GRIGORY

(nervously adjusting spectacles) Sorry, yes, sorry, sir, I would have, except we added the Politician and we removed the Lawyer - oh, I liked her - and then there was all that new research about influencers I had to do-

MAN

The tour group doesn’t need to hear your excuses, Grigory. Let’s get on with the show, chop chop, please!

GRIGORY

Yes yes yes! The show must go on, haha! The show, the show! Oh, where was I?

MAN

The landlord, Grigory.

GRIGORY

Ah! Now, the landlord is a fascinating creature: look at him, crouching, just about to knock on the tenant’s door! Landlords are not typically aggressive, unless provoked. Isn’t that right, buddy?

GRIGORY walks up to the LANDLORD and pats him on the shoulder. The LANDLORD doesn’t respond.

MAN

Ah. Now what if the landlord is provoked_

GRIGORY

Great question as always, sir!

GRIGORY starts pulling things out of his pocket - a swath of rainbow coloured cloth, a pocket watch, a Bible. He hands these items to an audience member.

GRIGORY

Could you hold this for me for a minute?

he finally pulls a dog plushie out of his pocket and throws it into the cage with the LANDLORD. The LANDLORD suddenly stands up and starts talking to the audience.

LANDLORD

There’s no pets allowed! This is very clearly stated in the lease agreement. No pets, no pets, no pets! Do you know how many people want this place? Do you, do you, do you? Do you know how easily I could get rid of you and the mutt?

MAN reaches into the cage and takes the plushie back, at which the LANDLORD sits back down, suddenly calm. While GRIGORY is speaking, MAN gives the dog plushie to an audience member “for learning purposes”.

GRIGORY

But an interesting observation is that when confronted with an animal not owned by the tenants, the landlord doesn’t typically become hostile.

GRIGORY takes another plushie out of his pocket - a rat - which he throws into the cage with the LANDLORD, who looks at it passively for a while before looking at the audience and speaking.

LANDLORD

Actually, as part of the natural flora and fauna of the place, the mould and the rats have to stay. I can’t help that I’ve a soft spot for preserving the natural biodiversity of this place.

MAN

What a fascinating specimen! Now come, Grigory, let’s move on and leave our little landlord to his rat.

The LANDLORD may, at this point, start gnawing on the rat.

MAN

Say, what could you tell our bright minds about this fellow over here?

GRIGORY and MAN move over to the POLITICIAN. He stands up and, in a monotone, almost bored voice, starts speaking.

POLITICIAN

My name is -

MAN

Jesus Christ! What do I pay all these trainers and tamers and tutors for when nobody here bothers to learn their damn lines?

GRIGORY

Ah, well, actually, sir, you ah, you stopped paying for any trainers a month ago. If I remember correctly, you mentioned something about them being… let me see, it’s in my notes… (pulls out piece of paper triumphantly) Aha! Worthless vermin that you couldn’t even display in the zoo if you dressed them up as parrots.

MAN

That was a rhetorical question, Grigory.

GRIGORY

Sorry sir!

MAN sighs, rubbing his face.

MAN

Let’s just get on with the presentation, shall we?

GRIGORY

Wonderful idea sir! Now, this particular exhibit is a politician.

MAN

Oh! Well that should raise everyone’s spirits, shouldn’t it? (rubbing his hands) Nothing like a little politics to get the blood pumping. Now, Grigory, why don’t you tell us a bit about this man’s policies.

GRIGORY

Oh, policies? No, I’m afraid he didn’t have any of those.

MAN

What do you mean he didn’t have any policies? He was a politician, wasn’t he?

GRIGORY

Right, yes, well, he did look mighty fine on camera, sir.

MAN

Oh Jesus. Well, ah, are there any questions from the tour group? No? Right, I trust we’re all learning something, taking notes - this is an educational environment first and foremost, after all. So, Grigory, if he didn’t have any policies, what information can you tell us about this wonderful exhibit?

 GRIGORY

Well… he’s a politician, sir. I’m not really sure what type of information you’re looking for.

MAN

What do I pay you for? Give me anything, for the love of God Grigory.

 GRIGORY

Right. Diet was… well, apparently he tried giving avocado on toast a go for a while to appeal to millennials. Took him a bit to realize that that’s a bygone era. Now he mostly eats whatever the voters think a politician should eat.

MAN

What’s that even mean? That makes no sense whatsoever. I swear Grigory, you’re getting worse and worse with each tour.

(to tour group) Apologies. This specimen is quite new. Maybe you’d like for him to say a few words, since Grigory is stumped? Yes? Perfect!

(to POLITICIAN) Alright old chap, up and at ‘em. Give us your big introduction.

The POLITICIAN stays silent, staring dumbly into space.

MAN

Well? Would you like to tell us about where you’re from, perhaps?

MAN grabs GRIGORY’S notes.

MAN

Says here you’ve an appetite for… Slavic women, is that correct? Two ex-wives? Maybe you’d like to introduce them? Talk about the good ol’ days at… Charvard?

(to GRIGORY) Is this a typo?

GRIGORY

Oh no no, sir. He studied business management at Charvard before dropping out to become a stock image model for a while, until he found his passion for politics.

Pause.

MAN

Oooh - kay. Okay. Well. I think it’s about time we move to another exhibit anyway, isn’t it?

POLITICIAN

My name is -

MAN

No! No, stop that, stop ruining my show, you goddamn…

MAN pauses and takes a deep breath before smiling at the audience.

MAN

Sorry for that, ladies and gents. We’ve found that passionate interaction with the specimen helps them not get bored. I mean, we’re a zoo, not a prison, hah! Grigory, throw the politician a toy, please.

GRIGORY is looking through his pockets for something while MAN is talking. He might ask the audience members for a toy or the items from his pocket back and throw it to the POLITICIAN.

MAN

Now, this one has been with us for a while. For any returning visitors, I’m sure you’re happy to see that this little creature is still happy and thriving.

MAN reaches over to ruffle the BANKERS hair. The BANKER looks bored and speaks in a condescending tone.

MAN

This is our Banker! He’s a bit of a strange one. Nobody really knows what he does.

BANKER

Actually, it’s very simple. I facilitate a number of financial transactions for my customers. I advise on savings, loans, overdraft...

MAN

Isn’t he peculiar! Look at him go - financial transactions. I mean, he’s just making up his own vocabulary, isn’t he?

“Overdraft”! I mean gosh, that’s just wonderful.

BANKER

An overdraft is a loan provided by the bank for expenses when there are insufficient funds in the customers bank account.

MAN

How fascinating! You’re like a little magicians, aren’t you? Just magic up all this money when your clients have none - and then when you use it up, POOF! And you can just get another one.

BANKER

That’s not -

MAN

Stocks, cash, drafts, words, words, words - why, you do like to talk, don’t you!

GRIGORY

Sir, should I - should I read my notes?

MAN

No, Grigory, I want you to feed them to the tour group - yes I want you to read your notes!

GRIGORY

Ah! Wonderful, sir. This is the banker!

MAN

We’ve been over that, Grigory. Do you happen to have any new information for us?

GRIGORY

Habitat….uh, their office. Alternatively, the bathroom of their office. Diet (scanning notes) cocaine off the bathroom of their office.

MAN

Are you sure you’re not mixing that up with the lawyer’s notes?

GRIGORY

No sir, the lawyers have moved to having cocaine off their desks.

MAN

Ah! (to tour group) Now the wonderful thing here is that even I learn something with each tour! I trust you’ve all -

There’s a BANG sound heard offstage.

GRIGORY

Oh, shit.

GRIGORY runs off to check and returns.

MAN

(to audience) I’m sure everything is perfectly all right.

GRIGORY returns, looking nervous.

MAN

Don’t tell me - is it PETA again? I told them I wouldn’t test my makeup on the specimen again, but -

GRIGORY

No, worse.

MAN

You don’t mean -

GRIGORY

It’s the lawyers in the other rooms, sir. They’re revolting.

MAN

Oh Jesus! Run, Grigory, run!

MAN starts picking up and taking any props around - signs on the cages, decorations, etc.

GRIGORY

But the zoo -

MAN

Fuck the zoo, Grigory! It’s a dog eat dog world out there!

BANKER

(bored) You’re using the phrase wrong.

MAN

I hope the dogs eat you first! Come, Grigory, come on!

MAN grabs GRIGORY and starts hauling him to the side.

GRIGORY

Oh, but I’ve collected so much information on these! I’ve grown so fond of them, Man!

MAN

What did I tell you about addressing me by my first name? Grigory, we can make another zoo wherever - there’s plenty of schmucks who’ll buy tickets anywhere.

(to tour group) Not you, you’ve been wonderful - what’s that, a refund? No, you can’t get a refund, I’m afraid, that was in the waiver you all signed -

Sounds like a mob running, crashes, angry voices, etc. can be heard from sidestage. Grigory runs from cage to cage, opening the cages of LANDLORD, POLITICIAN and BANKER while MAN is speaking.[a]

MAN

Right, well, it’s been wonderful guiding you, but we unfortunately have some business to attend to. Grigory - sic em!

GRIGORY pats the LANDLORD on the back.

GRIGORY

Go get ‘em, boys!

The LANDLORD, BANKER and POLITICIAN run, yelling, towards the sounds from stage left while GRIGORY and MAN run stage right as the curtain falls.

[a]Alternatively, it would end with grigory and man just running off and leaving the zoo animals in the cages as the curtain falls, but i'm not sure which version i prefer
